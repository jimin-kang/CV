Hello!

My name is Jimin Kang, and I am a student-athlete at Amherst College (c/o 2023) studying mathematics and computer science. Math and CS intrigue me because they are subjects where many concepts seem abstract initially, but with enough practice its applications for explaining phenomenon in everyday life become apparent. These subjects mix computational logic with creativity - given some intuitive facts, what conclusions can we reach? Studying math and CS has helped me improve my general problem solving skills - by understanding individual steps, you can end up solving much more complex puzzles than you may have anticipated.

Although my academic interests are mostly quantitative, I believe the most socially impactful work is done at the intersection of multiple disciplines. One of the main reasons I chose to attend Amherst is because its open liberal arts curriculum allows its students to take a variety of coursework in addition to major requirements - nights where I've had to write analyitical papers about philosophers such as Thomas Hobbes and then complete linear algebra problem sets have expanded my mind in a manner many other students don't have the opportunity to experience. My well-rounded education has equipped me with the critical thinking skills necessary to take on any role that requires solving complex problems to drive social impact.

Bio
